This table shows (at a high level) whether Turing machines with m states and n symbols are known or believed to be universal. “Weakly universal” means the machine can simulate any TM but may require a specially prepared (possibly infinite) initial tape. “Universal” (often “strongly universal”) means simulation is possible starting from a blank tape and a finite input. “Not universal” or “No known proof” indicates no proof of universality is known or widely accepted for that configuration.
